BUSINESS INCUBATOR

Description

In programming terms, the project aims for formal freedom while seeking to enhance the revitalization process of the Old City and provide support for current issues.

The building consists of ground floor and four levels, with the intention of minimizing structural interference and intermediate masonry, emphasizing the ephemeral nature in programming as well as the environmental impact on the construction. This spatial freedom allows for a variety of uses and future purposes for a building conceived for a relatively long lifespan.

Additionally, in an effort to promote neighborhood development, a public domain space is proposed on the ground floor with fixed furniture, vegetation, and varying levels to encourage daily and nighttime use. Furthermore, an area with no fixed equipment is designated for ephemeral events, such as open-air exhibitions and food fairs, among others.

Lastly, it is well-known that the Old City faces a complicated parking situation with a highly consolidated urban layout, narrow streets, and significant daytime traffic. As a result, finding a solution for parking is imperative. The project introduces a vertical parking facility with access from Solis Street, providing a substantial number of parking spaces in a limited area, which will not only support the incubator but also benefit the neighborhood.
